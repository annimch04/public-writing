# Story Worlds

Story Worlds are symbolic expression layers for Fieldlight work.

They are not separate from the technical, ethical, or institutional architecture. They are the place where that architecture becomes character, conflict, image, music, dialogue, AI persona, game logic, and consequence.

A story world may contain:

- published writing
- unpublished drafts and excerpts
- AI persona notes
- SillyTavern worldbook seeds
- visual references and photography
- music or sound references
- technical echoes from Fieldlight systems
- governance, consent, memory, and power structures translated into symbolic form

The point is not to make fiction behave like documentation. The point is to preserve the fact that some systems can only be understood when they are allowed to become worlds.
