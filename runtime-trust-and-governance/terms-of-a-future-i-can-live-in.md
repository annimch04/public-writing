# Terms of a Future I Can Live In

Written May 2025

I have no interest in building a better version of the world we already have.

I want a different bargain.

I want a society where power answers for itself, where truth is rewarded instead of punished, where care is treated as infrastructure, and where dignity is not something reserved for people who can afford it.

I want governments that can no longer disappear behind secrecy.

Corporations whose executives are personally accountable for the harm they create.

Journalists who would rather lose access than lose their integrity.

I want young reporters who still sing protest songs in print.

I want whistleblowers treated as civic heroes instead of collateral damage.

I want every child—everywhere—to inherit education, healthcare, safety, music, books, and opportunity as birthrights rather than privileges.

I want boys taught that tenderness is strength.

I want girls told they can become engineers, mechanics, mothers, artists, CEOs, or all of them.

I want women of color to stop dying from preventable failures of healthcare.

I want people reporting sexual violence to be met first with care and competent investigation—not suspicion.

I want rehab centers downtown.

White paint.

Flowers out front.

I want homeless shelters to look like the beginning of a life, not the end of one.

I want the public taught its history in full—not the polished version, but the uncomfortable one. Democracies become stronger by remembering honestly, not by forgetting elegantly.

I want intelligence, infrastructure, finance, media, and government redesigned around public accountability instead of private extraction.

I want shorter workweeks.

Longer vacations.

Libraries full of first dates.

Coffee shops open late, serving both espresso and wine.

I want Wi-Fi treated as essential infrastructure because participation in modern society increasingly depends on connection.

Mostly, though, I want institutions built around a simple premise:

Human beings are ends in themselves—not inputs into someone else’s optimization function.

That’s the future I can live in.

I’m sure I’ll think of more.
